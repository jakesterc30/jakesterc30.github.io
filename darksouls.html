Dark Souls

by Jacob Cano

Posted on December 15

Dark Souls, a game not for the faint-hearted. One of the most difficult RPG's in the genre, and maybe the most difficult, rage inducing game I have ever played. I haven't even beaten it yet! A classic in the RPG genre and a staple in the gaming community as a whole.

Another name for this game, which is entirely accurate, is Rage Quit Simulator. Dark Souls, especially the first one, is known for it's insane difficulty and crazy hard bosses. I personally had spent WEEKS attempting to kill one boss, then another few weeks attempting to find the next boss. I lost count of how many times I had rage quit this game during that time.

The legends say that the boss called Ornstein and Smough will destroy a persons will to have the game installed. That the mere thought of even attempting that boss again terrifies the player. I can't wait to fight it.

That's part of the beauty in Dark Souls however, spending countless hours trying to beat this one boss, and then the joy of finally accomplishing it, is a great feeling. And each boss is unique in it's own way, but the game still remains insanely difficult. The graphics are stunning and ahead of it's time, as you get beautiful high tower views and murky dungeons, and the music sets the mood very well. The lore is there if you want it, but I am not a huge lore junkie.

The only downside to Dark Souls is that it has low replayability. You could do multiple run throughs as different classes, but for the most part, only masochists replay this game. 8/10
